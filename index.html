📋 Cash Flow Reference Guide

Your professor's complete classification rules — indirect method, LABOR DAY, and all complex transactions

L
LIABILITIES — DUPLICATE
Same direction as balance change
↑ Increase → ADD ➕ to Net Income
↓ Decrease → SUBTRACT ➖ from Net Income
AP ↑ → AddAP ↓ → Sub DTL ↑ → AddITP ↑ → Add Wages Pay ↑ → Add
A
ASSETS — REVERSE
Opposite direction as balance change
↑ Increase → SUBTRACT ➖ from Net Income
↓ Decrease → ADD ➕ to Net Income
AR ↑ → SubAR ↓ → Add Inventory ↑ → SubDTA ↑ → Sub Prepaid ↓ → Add

🟢 Operating Activities

  • ✅ Net Income (starting point)
  • ➕ Depreciation / Amortization / ROU Amortization
  • ➕ Loss on sale of assets
  • ➖ Gain on sale of assets
  • ➕ Bond Discount Amortization
  • ➖ Bond Premium Amortization
  • ➕ Share-Based Compensation Expense
  • ➕ FVTNI Unrealized Loss
  • ➖ FVTNI Unrealized Gain
  • ❌ AFS Unrealized G/L — NO adjustment (in OCI)
  • ➖ Equity Method Undistributed Earnings
  • ➕ DTL Increase | ➖ DTL Decrease
  • ➖ DTA Increase | ➕ DTA Decrease
  • ➕ Finance Lease Interest Payment
  • Working capital changes → LABOR DAY
  • ✅ Interest PAID = Operating
  • ✅ Dividends RECEIVED = Operating
  • ✅ Trading securities = Operating

🔵 Investing Activities

  • ✅ Purchase of PP&E (outflow)
  • ✅ Proceeds from sale of PP&E (inflow)
  • ✅ Purchase of AFS investments (outflow)
  • ✅ Proceeds from sale of AFS investments (inflow)
  • ✅ Purchase of HTM investments (outflow)
  • ✅ Loans made to others (outflow)
  • ✅ Collections on loans (inflow)
  • ✅ Purchase of equity method investments (outflow)
  • ❌ Trading securities → NOT here (Operating!)
  • ❌ Interest received → NOT here (Operating!)
  • ❌ Dividends received → NOT here (Operating!)
  • ⚠️ Full proceeds from asset sale go here
  • ⚠️ Gain/loss stays in Operating as adjustment

🟣 Financing Activities

  • ✅ Proceeds from bond issuance (inflow)
  • ✅ Repayment of bonds/notes (outflow)
  • ✅ Proceeds from stock issuance (inflow)
  • ✅ Treasury stock purchases (outflow)
  • ✅ Dividends PAID (outflow)
  • ✅ Finance lease principal payments (outflow)
  • ❌ Interest PAID → NOT here (Operating!)
  • ❌ Dividends PAID → NOT Operating (Financing!)
  • ⚠️ Bond issuance = actual cash proceeds received
  • ⚠️ Finance lease: split interest (Op) vs. principal (Fin)

🟠 Non-Cash Disclosures

  • ✅ Acquiring equipment via note payable
  • ✅ Acquiring ROU asset via finance lease
  • ✅ Converting bonds to common stock
  • ✅ Stock dividends declared
  • ✅ Acquiring land by issuing stock
  • ✅ Refinancing debt (non-cash portion)
  • ❌ These do NOT appear on the statement itself
  • ⚠️ Supplemental schedule disclosure only
  • ⚠️ Significant investing + financing with no cash
📊 Complex Transactions — Your Professor's Examples
📊 Last Week of Class Inc. — $400M Bond at Discount: Bond issuance proceeds ($380M) = Financing inflow. Interest paid ($18M cash) = Operating outflow. Discount amortization ($1M) = ADD BACK in operating. Principal at maturity ($400M) = Financing outflow.
📊 Making It to Finals Co. — Equity Method: Own 40% of Fire Z. Fire Z NI=$100K, Dividends=$30K. Investment Revenue=$40K. Dividends received=$12K. Undistributed earnings=$28K → SUBTRACT from NI (Asset Reverses — LABOR DAY).
📊 Barely Making It Co. — FVTNI vs. AFS: FVTNI unrealized loss → ADD BACK in operating (it's in NI but non-cash). AFS unrealized loss → NO adjustment (it's in OCI, never touched NI).
📊 Summer Freedom Venture — $3M Finance Lease: 1st payment (day 1) = $195,774 all Financing. 2nd payment = $84,127 Operating (interest) + $111,647 Financing (principal). ROU asset acquisition = $3M non-cash disclosure.
💡 Indirect vs. Direct Method: Both produce the SAME net cash from operating activities. Indirect starts with Net Income and works backwards. Direct converts each income statement item from accrual to cash basis. Your professor teaches the indirect method — that's what's on the exam!
⚠️ Top 5 Exam Traps: (1) Dividends PAID = Financing, NOT Operating. (2) Interest PAID = Operating, NOT Financing. (3) Trading securities = Operating, NOT Investing. (4) AFS unrealized G/L = NO cash flow adjustment. (5) Gain on sale → SUBTRACT from NI (full proceeds go to Investing).
